QUESTION 1 [10 Marks Total]
(a) State Henry's Law concerning the solubility of a gas in a liquid.

(b) Calculate the pH of a 0.05 $mol/dm^3$ solution of $H_2SO_4$, assuming complete ionization.
Part (a) Answer:
Henry's law states that at a constant temperature, the amount of a given gas that dissolves in a given type and volume of liquid is directly proportional to the partial pressure of that gas in equilibrium with that liquid. 2 MARKS
Part (b) Solution:
Since $H_2SO_4$ is a diprotic acid: $$[H^+] = 2 \times [H_2SO_4] = 2 \times 0.05 = 0.1 \, mol/dm^3$$ 2 MARKS - Substitution

Using the pH formula: $$pH = -\log[H^+]$$ $$pH = -\log(0.1) = 1$$ 1 MARK - Final Answer
